## Purpose

This file tracks external developments that could move the project's **biological bottlenecks**. It is deliberately kept **separate from the main document**.

**Fold-in rule (provenance gate):** an item enters the main document — as a new version, with proper attribution and the correct epistemic tag — **only when it becomes a verified deliverable** (a published result, a cleared device, a reproducible method), *not* when it is announced, promised, or hoped for. Items here carry **no** provenance weight until that gate is passed. This prevents news and optimism from contaminating the fact-spine.

Tags follow the document convention (`[ESTABLISHED]`, `[THEORETICAL]`, `[SPECULATIVE]`, etc.). A `[WATCHING]` marker here means "no deliverable yet; tracking only."

## Standing caveat (so hope stays calibrated)

Protein-structure prediction (AlphaFold lineage) **accelerates** the work biologists do; it does **not bypass** the tissue-level problems. Specifically, it does not by itself fix **microvascular degradation** (capillary deterioration — the real heart of the perfusion ceiling) or reverse **neuronal age**. A new tool is a faster route to a candidate, still requiring lab and clinical validation. Keep expectations on "accelerant," not "silver bullet."

## Rejected routes (do not re-add — see main doc §9)

- In-utero / pre-consent modification of a human to prepare for later brain transfer — **permanently rejected** (creates a person as an instrument without consent; also does not address bottleneck #2). The legitimate path to the same goal is W3 (consenting adults / tissue-level).
